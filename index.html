Team Work Policy

Remote Development Team • Effective March 2026

01

How We Work

SetBuddy is a fully remote team. There is no office and no requirement to be in a specific location. What matters is that work gets done on time and communication stays clear.

02

Availability & Communication

  • Be reachable on the team's primary channel (Discord/Slack) during your committed hours. You don't need to respond instantly, but aim for within 2 hours during those hours.
  • If you're going to be unavailable for a day or more, post a heads-up in the team channel before you go offline. No explanation needed — just a timeline.
  • Weekly check-ins happen every Sunday evening. Attendance is expected unless you've communicated in advance. Format: what you did, what's next, what's blocking you.
03

Tasks & Deadlines

  • Tasks are assigned through the project board (GitHub Projects, Notion, or whatever the team is using). Each task includes a description, priority level, and a due date.
  • Due dates are set collaboratively. If a deadline feels unrealistic, speak up when the task is assigned — not the day it's due. Renegotiating early is fine; missing silently is not.
  • If you're going to miss a deadline, flag it at least 24 hours in advance with a revised estimate.
  • Standard turnaround is 3–5 days for most tasks. Urgent tasks are marked and should be addressed within 24–48 hrs.
04

Code & Quality Standards

  • All code goes through pull requests. No direct pushes to main.
  • PRs need at least one review before merging. Keep PRs small and focused — one feature or fix per PR.
  • Write clear commit messages. If someone reads the log in 6 months, they should understand what changed and why.
  • If you break something, own it and fix it. No blame — just solutions.
05

Accountability

This is a small team. Everyone's contribution is visible and everyone's absence is felt.

  1. Do what you said you'd do, by when you said you'd do it.
  2. If you can't, communicate early.
  3. If a pattern of missed deadlines or unresponsiveness develops, the team lead will have a direct conversation. If it continues, your role on the project will be reconsidered.
06

Respect & Culture

  • Disagree openly, but never personally. Critique the code or the idea, not the person.
  • Everyone's time is valuable. Come to meetings prepared. Keep async messages concise.
  • Credit contributions publicly. If someone helped you solve a problem, say so.
